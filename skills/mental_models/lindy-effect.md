# Lindy Effect

The longer a non-perishable thing has survived, the longer its expected remaining lifespan. Future life expectancy is proportional to current age.

## Application

- **Technology choices:** a library maintained for 10 years will likely outlast one released 6 months ago
- **Practices:** programming patterns that have persisted for decades (UNIX philosophy, relational databases) are likely to persist for decades more
- **Books/ideas:** classics remain relevant precisely because they've already survived selection pressure
- **Architecture:** proven patterns (REST, message queues, relational DBs) are lower risk than novel approaches
